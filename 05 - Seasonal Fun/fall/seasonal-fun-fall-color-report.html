Fall Color Hunting

Best Bets

Even though the leaves have been falling around Asheville this week, there is still plenty of color to be seen, especially in the brilliant yellow of the ginkgo trees along Wall Street in Downtown. The Polk County area near Tryon and Saluda is also a good bet, as is the area around Chimney Rock and Lake Lure where color has progressed to the lower elevations. Check out our drive of the week for a scenic day trip that includes both. North of Weaverville along I-26, the leaves are mostly past peak, but beautiful reds and oranges are still visible along the lower flanks of the mountain ranges.

Here are other updates from around the mountains:

Biltmore

Fall color is finishing up for the year. Color is getting spotty with the dark red Red Oaks along estate roads and in the Deer Park. The last of the Sugar Maples are adding yellow and orange, and Sweet Gums, always one of the longest lasting fall color trees, are showing a blend of orange to maroon leaves on the trees along the Swannanoa River. In the gardens, Japanese Maples are highlighting the end of the season with every imaginable fall color, and a few Beeches and Hickories are holding onto their yellow leaves for a few more days.


Chimney Rock, Fall 2013

Chimney Rock State Park

Leaf colors exploded at Chimney Rock State Park last week, surrounding the area in brilliant golds, reds and oranges. Peak leaf colors arrived late last week, and the mountains should remain colorful into mid-November if the weather remains calm. The maples are stunning this year, and the red oaks are just now living up to their name. Magnolia trees add a rich chocolate brown to the layers of color. Leaf peepers can find beautiful autumn foliage along the Park's four scenic trails, or try out a rock climbing adventure for a new perspective on autumn.

Top Drives for the Week

Head to the towns of Saluda, Tryon and Lake Lure on this two-hour loop where fall color is still in full effect, or try a couple of out-and-back trips, stopping in Chimney Rock or Tryon the browse in shops and cafes.

  • From downtown Asheville head west on I-240 to I-26 E
  • Drive 26.2 miles and then take exit 54 for US-25 S
  • After 1.7 miles take the US 176 ramp to U.S. 25 N/Saluda/East Flat Rock/Hendersonville
  • Keep left at the fork and follow signs for 176 E
  • Turn left onto US-176/ Spartanburg Hwy.
  • Follow for 13.4 miles and then turn right onto Mc Cown St. and then turn left onto US-176/S Trade St.
  • Continue down US-176 for 8.4 miles until you reach Saluda.
  • From US-176 you will take a sharp left onto N Carolina 108 East/Lynn Rd.
  • Follow NC-108 and then turn left onto NC–9 North.
  • Follow NC-9 for 9 miles and then turn left onto US-64W/US-74 Alt W.
  • Continue to follow US-74 back into Asheville.

Fall Hike of the Week

Pearson's Falls

This natural heritage site is located five miles past Tryon on US-176. Named for its 90-foot waterfall there is a picnic area and hiking trail available to the public for a $5 usage fee.
