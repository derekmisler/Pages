Information regarding the impact of the federal government shutdown on area travel

Visitors to the Asheville area during the fall foliage season will be able to enjoy the scenic vistas and changing color throughout autumn, however, some additional planning may be required to eliminate any surprises. Despite the government shutdown, travelers can still enjoy the scenic views of the Blue Ridge Parkway, and US 441 through the Great Smoky Mountains National Park remains open.

  • Blue Ridge Parkway

    The Blue Ridge Parkway remains open to drivers and visitors at this time. Trailheads, overlooks and parking areas are also open, except those that are accessed through a campground. All visitor centers, campgrounds and restroom facilities have been closed, including the Parkway Visitor Center. For longer trips, visitors are encouraged to plan along the way and take advantage of the towns and communities that border the length of the Parkway. Additional information can be found on the Blue Ridge Parkway Association's website. For information on scenic drives and overlooks, area accommodations and other fall attractions and activities, please visit our Fall Seasonal Fun pages and listings.

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park

    US 441 through the park remains open to vehicles. However, parking and picnic area, campgrounds and visitor centers are closed. The gated areas to Cades Cove and Cataloochee area also closed.

  • National Forests

    This region is home to the Pisgah National Forest. Trails in the forests continue to be accessible, however, campgrounds and attractions that are on forest lands, such as the Cradle of Foresty on US 276 are closed at this time. Looking Glass Falls, the Pisgah Center for Wildlife Education and other attractions along US 276 are still open and accessible to the public.

  • Carl Sandburg Home

    The Carl Sandburg Home, a national historic site, located in Flat Rock, approximately 35 minutes south of Asheville, will be closed during the government shutdown.

NOTE: All North Carolina state-operated facilities, including Grandfather Mountain, Chimney Rock State Park, Mount Mitchell State Park and the North Carolina Arboretum, remain open to visitors. The Folk Art Center and all NC Welcome Centers and rest areas are also open to the public traveling highways throughout the state.

Visitor Resources

  • The Asheville Visitor Center at 36 Montford Avenue is open Monday–Friday from 8:30 a.m.–5:30 p.m. and on weekends from 9 a.m.–5 p.m., or call (828) 258-6129.
  • The Pack Square Park Visitor Pavilion in downtown Asheville is open daily from 9 a.m.–5 p.m.
  • For planning travel along the Blue Ridge Parkway, visit the Blue Ridge Parkway Association at www.blueridgeparkway.org.
  • For information about Great Smoky Mountains National Park, contact the Friends of the Smokies at www.friendsofthesmokies.org.
