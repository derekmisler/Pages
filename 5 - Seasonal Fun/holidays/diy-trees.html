  • If you choose to buy a Christmas tree from a lot, ask the retailer when the tree was harvested. Choose the freshest tree available.

  • Bounce the tree on its trunk and observe the amount of needle drop. While it's common for dead needles to fall, you should avoid a tree which exhibits a great deal of green needles dropping.

  • Another way to test freshness is to run your hand along the tree branches to check for falling needles.

  • If you decide to cut your own tree, be aware that many Christmas tree farms will assist you with selecting and cutting your tree. Some farms even have such additional activities as wagon rides for children.

  • To get the most from your tree, put it in water is soon as you get home. If you purchased the tree from a lot, ask for a fresh-cut to be made, otherwise the tree will not absorb water.

  • For the brightest look, stand the tree in a pail of water, hose off the branches to wash out dead needles and clean all dust and webs.

  • Check water daily, and replenish as soon as it runs low. The tree will drink up to a gallon of water a day.
