This is your official source for the 2013 spring bloom report in Asheville and Western North Carolina. Each week we'll tell you where spring blooms are most prevalent in the mountains. Be sure to visit this page weekly for the most current spring updates.

Spring Bloom Hunting

The wildflower scenery is in full swing now! This week, go blooms hunting at Chimney Rock Park. With the recent rain, the vegetation looks bright green. Fire Pinks are now blooming, along with purple phacelia, wild columbine and Little Sweet Betsy trillium. Magnolias are adding color to the beautiful and abundant flowering dogwoods, silverbell trees, sweetshrub and Carolina rhododendron. This Saturday you can sign up for their guided Spring Wildflower Walk this Sat., May 11 from 11am–noon.

Treat mom to a special day in the mountainswith one of these deals just for her.

  • Chimney Rock Park is offering free admission this weekend (May 11-12), for mothers only. Buy one regularly-priced adult ticket and receive one free. Enjoy a free live concert in the Meadows from noon-3pm. You'll need to present this coupon.

  • Mom's Admitted Free at Biltmore: On May 12, all mother's get in for free with the purchase of a youth or adult ticket. Enjoy the progression of flowers at Biltmore Blooms, music on the bandstand at Antler Hill Village (4–8:45p.m.), and a grape wine stomp at the winery from 2–5p.m.
