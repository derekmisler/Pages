SPRING BLOOM HUNTING

© Biltmore Company

Asheville's city's streets are lined with frothy blooming ornamental fruit trees like Bradford Pears and cherries. Yards and public plantings are bright with quince, flowering almond and forsythia. This is also when you'll find beds of blooming daffodils in the area's many gardens. Keep an eye out for naturalized daffodils in woodland areas on Biltmore Estate and The Blue Ridge Parkway.

Flocks of daffodils in varying sizes, shapes and forms abound at the North Carolina Arboretum. There are 12 categories of daffodils from fragrant, delicate and small to bold and audacious. Plantings at the Arboretum make the most of what these perennial favorites have to offer.

Also look for colorful and sometimes fragrant plantings of daffodils, hyacinths, violas, hellebores, moss phlox, veronica in the English Walled Garden this week at Biltmore. Along the Approach Road, look for Winter Honeysuckle, Cornell Pink Azalea, Redbud, and Spice Bush. The unmistakable purple blooms of PJM, the earliest rhododendron of the year, provide bright spots of color at the Inn and Antler Village.
