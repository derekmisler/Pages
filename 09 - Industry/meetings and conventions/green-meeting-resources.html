Below, you'll find green meeting resources that will help you plan a more sustainable event in Asheville. Reducing the environmental impact of guests on the beautiful mountains of Western North Carolina is a fundamental objective of the entire Asheville hospitality community. It is part of the region's heritage and is embraced as part of the brand that makes Asheville one of the truly special places in the world to visit.

The Asheville Offset

The Asheville Offset offers an opportunity for the attendees of meetings and conventions to directly impact the sustainability and social well-being of their host city.

  • The Asheville Offset is an opportunity to contribute (per attendee or per group) to the Insulate! program of Asheville’s Warren Wilson College.
  • Meeting attendees may choose to purchase an offset to mitigate environmental impacts of their travel and activities while in Asheville. All of the money goes directly back into the local community through the Insulate! program, which weatherizes the homes of citizens below the poverty level, free of charge, thus reducing utility bills, energy usage and corresponding carbon emission. The offset donation is flexible and we work with each group on their individual commitment.
  • People below the poverty line can spend more than 1/3 of their total annual income to heat and cool their house. Weatherizing can create as much as a 30% reduction in energy used. The homes are owned by seniors who have requested assistance through the Buncombe County Council on Aging. Student volunteers carry out the work and conduct pre- and post- energy audits and monitoring of the homes.
  • Warren Wilson College is a national leader in sustainable education and stewardship, and its Insulate! program has been duplicated across the nation.
Natural beauty is part of Asheville and our local culture is infused with the desire to celebrate and protect it. Groups come here for that connection and now they can take part in it with a simple, yet mindful, offset donation. The Asheville Offset allows attendees to reduce their carbon footprint in a way that directly reduces energy consumption on a local level in Asheville.

Community Service Oranizations

Contact these organizations for information on planning a group service project:

  • Asheville Greenworks, an affiliate of Keep America Beautiful, is a volunteer-based organization working to achieve a Clean and Green Asheville and Buncombe County through community organizing, educating and environmental stewardship 828.254.1776
  • Hands on Asheville-Buncombe makes it easy to find a one-time volunteer project for your group. Volunteering as a group is a great way for companies and groups to promote teamwork, build morale, develop leadership and strengthen community relations...not to mention it's a great way to have fun, give back, and learn more about the Asheville community.
  • MANNA Food Bank is a registered 501©(3) nonprofit organization that collects, stores, warehouses and distributes food to MANNA accredited non profits throughout 16 counties in Western North Carolina. 828-299-3663
  • RiverLink is a regional non-profit spearheading the economic and environmental revitalization of the French Broad River. Make a difference by participating in a river cleanup with RiverLink and Adopt-a-Stream teams. 828-252-8474 ext. 118, or volunteer@riverlink.org
  • Asheville Area Habitat for Humanity provides single-day team building opportunities for groups of 6-12 on its construction sites. "Blueprint" sponsors donate $1,000 towards the project cost. The group receives t-shirts (for all participants), recognition on-site and on-line, a digital photograph of your group on site, and more. Help Habitat help families in need of safe, decent housing. Contact Betsy Warren, Sponsorship Coordinator at (828) 210-9363 or bwarren@ashevillehabitat.org.

No time on the agenda for a project? Donate.

Donating to local charities is also a wonderful way to benefit your host community. Give attendees the chance to donate a few dollars at registration, or donate unused and leftover items from your meeting. Here are some organizations that can facilitate donating:

  • Second Bloomcollects perfectly good flowers that are headed to the landfill and spreads them around to appreciative residents in nursing homes, rehab centers and Hospice. Call 828.252.4232 to make a donation.
  • Western Carolina Rescue Ministries accepts food donations. Call 828-254-0471 to donate leftover food. Mon-Fri only.
  • The Hope Chest for Women, a WNC nonprofit, makes the most of in-kind donations of local gas and pharmacy gift cards, gift certificates and monetary contributions. These items help offset the expense of transportation, prescription and supplemental medications, and other necessities for women diagnosed with breast or gynecologic cancer. Call 828.418.1344 for more information.

Get Creative

Art supplies for local schools, pet food & supplies, gloves and hats for needy children, canned food items for local food bank or homeless shelters will make interesting table decorations.
