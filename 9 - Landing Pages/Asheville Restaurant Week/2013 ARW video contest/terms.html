Eligibility and Judging

There will be two rounds of voting. Who picks the winners in round one? You do! And your friends—plus about 43,300 of Asheville's closest fans.

The top ten photos from viewer's choice will undergo a final vote by the judging panel at the Asheville Convention and Visitor's Bureau (ACVB). They will ultimately choose the grand prize winner.

All photos are pre-screened. Photos depicting nudity, profanity, commercial or political interests, or anything wildly off-topic are strictly prohibited. The Asheville CVB reserves the right to disqualify any photographs deemed inappropriate.

If you choose to include people in your submission, you are responsible for obtaining the necessary releases from the individuals depicted, and must be able to provide copies of those releases to the ACVB upon request.

Submissions may be watermarked if desired, but the watermark should be inconspicuous enough to not interfere with the judging of the photo. A watermarked photo might not be honored by the competition (at the judges sole discretion) if the photographer is unable or unavailable to provide a pristine, watermark-free version of the image when asked.

Images will be judged on originality, technical excellence, composition, overall impact and artistic merit. The judges are a panel of ACVB staff members familiar with photography and responsible for selecting images for various Asheville's printed and online marketing.

The Asheville CVB shall determine winner eligibility in its sole and absolute discretion. All decisions made by the judges are final.

Deadlines

Online entries can be uploaded starting 12:01 pm Eastern Standard Time on October 1, 2012.
Deadline for submitting entries is 11:59 pm Pacific Standard Time on October 28, 2012.

Entrants may be asked to supplement their submission (such as a paragraph about their photo, the highest resolution version of their photo, or a watermark-free version of their photo) anytime on or after October 29, 2012. Public voting for the grand-prize winner will be held for at least a two-week period to begin October 29, 2012 and ending November 11. Grand Prize Winner and Runners Up will be announced in mid-November 2012. There is a limit of one vote per person for each photo.

The Asheville CVB reserves the right to adjust any deadline(s) as the result of causes beyond its immediate control.

YOUR RIGHTS

You will retain all rights to any photograph you submit—including ownership if applicable. You only grant The ACVB rights (as described below) to your photograph. The ACVB will contact you to confirm that you wish to grant us rights to your image as described below.

By entering the contest you grant The Asheville CVB a royalty-free, nonexclusive right, in perpetuity, to:

  • Use the photograph on the Internet.
  • Use the photograph in The Asheville CVB's non-commercial communications.
  • Provide your photograph to other individuals and organizations for non-commercial use, Asheville-related use in news stories, newsletters, reports, slide shows, displays, web pages, and the like.
  • Use, in connection with the Photo Contest, your name, city, state and country of residence in promotions and other publications.
  • Keep the files provided, and to archive the images on CD or in other electronic forms, so that your photos can be properly credited.
