Nestled in a verdant Blue Ridge Mountain valley and framed by the highest peaks along the Blue Ridge Parkway, Asheville, NC has become the craft-brewing center of the Southeast. Asheville beer has garnered so much enthusiasm that it was named the winner of Examiner's "Beer City, USA" poll for four years.

Boasting more breweries per capita than any U.S. city, the Asheville area is home to 23 craft breweries. On any given day, roughly 100 local beers can be enjoyed in Asheville, served on draft and in bottles. The scene has reached a fever pitch with five annual beer festivals, brewery tours and unusual products made from beer like shampoo, ice cream and mustard.

With 15 active farmers' markets, 250+ independent restaurants, 23 fantastic craft breweries, America's most visited winery and hundreds of inspired local products—all within a city of 85,000—Asheville is a thriving community of culinary creatives. The city gleefully blurs the lines between art, food and mountain culture, pushing the boundaries of the plate and charting new territory in craft beer, cocktails and wine. Local sake or hard cider anyone? Grab a chair and join us…

Legendary Conference Locale: Biltmore

Conference programming will take place on the grounds of America's largest private home—Biltmore. The 250-room mansion, built by George W. Vanderbilt in 1895 and beautifully preserved and restored for visitors today, is only one part of the Biltmore experience, which also includes numerous gardens, trails and outdoor adventures on the 8,000-acre estate, shopping and dining at Antler Hill Village, and welcoming hospitality at the Inn on Biltmore Estate. Add to that a legacy of sustainable agricultural practices, America's most visited (and award-winning) winery, and the majestic vistas of the surrounding Blue Ridge Mountains, and it's easy to see why more than one million travelers choose to visit Biltmore each year.

Meet the Foodtopians:
Farm Tour & Tasting Event

The Food Blog Forum continues August 19 with a gourmet adventure and tasting event showcasing the flavors of Foodtopia, hosted by Explore Asheville. Click here to register.

  • 10:30AM–10:00PM—MEET THE FOODTOPIANS: Tour departs promptly at 10:30 from Doubletree Biltmore. Handcrafted cheese, craft beer, wood-fired bread, cooking demos and mountainside berry picking are just a few of the tasty adventures to experience on this day-long tasting and tour event brought to you by Explore Asheville and spanning the local food, farm, independent restaurant and brewing culture.
  • 10:30AM–2PM—TASTE & SIP: Experience the flavors of Asheville at a tasting event at the legendary Highland Brewing Company, celebrating 20 years as Asheville's first brewery. The event showcases tastes from some of the city's best restaurants and provides the opportunity to interact with local Asheville food personalities. Brewery tours also available.
  • 2–5:30PM—BEYOND FARM TOURS: Join us on an outing to nearby farms, Hickory Nut Gap Farm and Looking Glass Creamery for tours, tastings, demonstrations and special guests that include local artisan food producers. Hickory Nut Gap Farm's fourth generation farmers raise 100% grass-fed animals for over 30 restaurants and Looking Glass Creamery, also family owned and operated, is an award-winning producer of handmade cheese and traditional goat's milk caramel.
  • 5:30–6:30PM—SHORT BREAK: Freshen up back at the hotel for an evening downtown.
  • 6:30–10PM—INDIE RESTAURANTS & ART DECO DOWNTOWN: With more art deco than any other southeastern city outside Miami, downtown Asheville makes a colorful backdrop for a casual tour of Asheville's independent restaurant scene. Follow the provided map or join a local expert as you explore Asheville eateries offering Food Blog Forum specials and tastings via the Asheville Independent Restaurants Association.

• This is an all-day event; transportation will be provided to/from the farms and venues, departing and returning to Doubletree Biltmore.
• Please wear comfortable clothes, sunscreen and walking shoes for exploring life on these working farms.
• These farms are not wheelchair accessible, so please contact us if this is an issue.
