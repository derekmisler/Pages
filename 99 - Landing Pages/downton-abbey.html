This season in Asheville, North Carolina, America's Largest Home takes on one of television's biggest dramas. Beginning February 5, Biltmore welcomes 47 original costumes from the hit PBS series Downton Abbey as a part of the exhibit "Dressing Downton: Changing Fashion for Changing Times."

The exhibit of award-winning costumes-all of which were actually worn on the set of the show-juxtapose Downton's fictional Crawley family and household with the real life stories of the Vanderbilts, highlighting the striking resemblance between Biltmore and the way of life at Highclere Castle around the turn of twentieth century.

The display explores themes such as the evolution of fashion, nuances of etiquette, the changing roles of women and the life of service staff. New stories about George and Edith Vanderbilt, their daughter Cornelia, their often illustrious guests and their domestic servants will be shared in Biltmore's free printed guide and in the Biltmore House audio guide ($10 in addition to estate admission.)

Experience a Taste of Downton

Upstairs-Downstairs Tour: Descend the back stairs at Biltmore for a sneak peek of servant life in the time of the Vanderbilts. Visit areas managed by the butler, housekeeper and Edith Vanderbilt's lady's maid, then enter some of the home's best guest rooms for stories of grand house parties and famous friends. The price of this specialty tour is $17 in addition to estate admission and requires advance reservations.

Afternoon Tea at the Library Lounge: Prep your pinkies for this traditional English tea, served at the Inn on Biltmore Estate with a breathtaking view of the grounds. Menu includes finger sandwiches, scones, breads, cheeses and pastries.

Lex18, a 1920s-style speakeasy in downtown, offers a similar menu for afternoon tea, which is also accompanied by a glass of champagne, live classical music and a brief history of tea etiquette.

From Brunch to Bubbles: If it's too early for tea, turn to a special English Sunday Brunch at Cedric's Tavern, located at the Estate's Antler Hill Village.

And beginning in April, save the date for Vanderbilt Dinner Theater, based on the theatrical dinner parties that Edith Vanderbilt hosted in the 1920s. Each event includes an hors d'oeuvres and wine reception followed by a four-course plated dinner and live entertainment and will show off one of the estate's unique venues, including the champagne cellar and Biltmore's rooftop.

Downton Hotel Packages: Live like a Vanderbilt (or a Crawley) during your visit with the "Dressing Downton" package at the Inn on Biltmore Estate. In addition to house admission and accommodations at this four-diamond hotel, guests will also enjoy daily buffet breakfast, afternoon tea service, the Biltmore House audio tour, a souvenir guidebook and other amenities.

Or check in to the DoubleTree by Hilton Asheville Biltmore with the Downton Abbey Experience package, which includes accommodations, daily breakfast, Biltmore tickets and audio tours, the official "Dressing Downton" tour book and a selection of English teas and homemade biscuits upon arrival. Hotel guests may also purchase exclusive Downton-themed merchandise and enjoy an English-inspired in-room dining menu during the exhibit.

The Carolina Bed & Breakfast, a Gilded Age gem built by Biltmore architect Richard Sharp Smith, also offers package deals for Downton including tickets to Biltmore, the Upstairs-Downstairs Tour and Afternoon Tea on the estate.
