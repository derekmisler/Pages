Discover Asheville's music scene for yourself.

 
"Scores of talented acoustic musicians call Asheville home. For big-name acts, check out the schedule at the Orange Peel Social Aid and Pleasure Club, where Bob Dylan, Sonic Youth and, more recently, Smashing Pumpkins have played."
- New York Times
Asheville's thriving music scene means that literally hundreds of bands and musicians make their way through the city during the year. The best way to tap into Asheville's live music is to experience it first-hand at one of our local venues.

Popular Music Venues in Downtown Asheville

  • Asheville Civic Center is home to a variety of entertainment events in downtown Asheville. The space frequently serves as the home for large performances from nationally known musicians. More...
     
  • BoBo Gallery is an art gallery, bar and music venue rolled into one location. Music ranges from jazz to electronic to world music and usually starts later in the evening.
     
  • Diana Wortham Theatre at Pack Place offers an intimate setting for music performances by national and local artists. Located in the center of Asheville's downtown art scene, it offers a variety of acts throughout the year. More...
     
  • Ed Boudreaux's Bayou BBQ is a restaurant by trade, but offers live music in the evenings. More...
     
  • Emerald Lounge stays true to its bohemian location by booking roots, reggae, funk, jazz and hip-hop acts.
     
  • Fred's Speakeasy is a small basement club in downtown that features all genres of music. Shows are Thursday through Saturday.
     
  • French Broad Brewery is home to one of Asheville's microbreweries, but also offers live music from local musicians in its tasting room in the evenings. More...
     
  • The Garage at Biltmore offers late night shows and is nestled in the same building as French Broad Brewery and MonteVecchia Music and Arts Studio. More...
     
  • The Grey Eagle, located in Asheville's River Arts District, offers a great listening-room vibe and excellent sound system. Musical guests often include singer-songwriters and bands on the cusp of making it big. More...
     
  • Hannah Flanagan's Irish Pub & Eatery lets patrons jam out to blues and rock Thursday through Saturday nights. More...
     
  • Jack of the Wood Pub, a popular local hangout, offers live music throughout the week. Sunday night is Irish music, Wednesday night is old-time mountain music and Thursday night is bluegrass jam. More...
     
  • Jerusalem Garden is a Moroccan and Mediterranean restaurant that serves up live music Thursday through Saturday. More...
     
  • Lobster Trap offers live blues, jazz and acoustic music alongside its famous seafood and home-brewed beer. More...
     
  • The Orange Peel was voted as one of Rolling Stone magazine's "Top Five Rock Clubs in the Country." This venue host national acts from all genres. More...
     
  • Stella Blue books a little bit of everything in a setting that's sexy and sleek. Music includes blues, jazz, reggae, techno and hip-hop. More...
     
  • Thomas Wolfe Auditorium is located in the heart of downtown Asheville. It hosts a variety of musical acts ranging from the local symphony to opera to concerts. More...
     
  • Vincenzo's Bistro serves up live jazz, blues and classic standards every night in addition to its delicious menu. More...
     
  • Zambra tempts guests with delicious tapas, but also offers light jazz and world music in the evenings. More...

Other Local Venues

  • Feed & Seed is a popular family venue located just 10 minutes outside of downtown Asheville. Bluegrass and Americana acts perform Friday and Saturday nights.
     
  • Town Pump Tavern is located down the road in nearby Black Mountain and has featured local, regional and national acts for over 25 years. Performances range from Americana, bluegrass, blues, honky-tonk and rock.
     
  • White Horse Black Mountain is located just minutes outside of Asheville in neighboring Black Mountain. This spot brings in unique bands off the radar and local musicians.
