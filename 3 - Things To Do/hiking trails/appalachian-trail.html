The globally iconic Appalachian National Scenic Trail stretches 2,180 miles from Georgia to Maine. This "super-trail" of the Appalachian Mountains can be accessed at many locations within 45 min to 2 hours of Asheville. Much of this white-blazed Trail is on ridgeline and affords spectacular views on clear days. Below are some hiking and view options. Additional information about the Trail and an interactive map can be found on the website of the Appalachian Trail Conservancy.

Charlies Bunion

  • Extraordinary views from this scalable stone outcrop in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.
  • Length: 8.8 mi. total
  • Elevation gain: 1640'
  • Trail type: in and out (white blaze)
  • Skill level: moderate to strenuous
  • Trailhead elevation: 5046'

Directions from Asheville:

  1. I-40 west, 18 mi.
  2. Exit 27 US-74 west toward Maggie Valley, 4 mi.
  3. Exit 103 US-19 through Maggie Valley, 12 mi.
  4. Turn right on Blue Ridge Parkway ramp, left on BRP, 13 mi.
  5. Turn right on US-441, to Newfound Gap parking lot on right, 16 mi.
  6. A.T. trailhead is to right of restrooms.

Clingmans Dome

  • Highest point on the Appalachian Trail and in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Views from the observation tower are 360° and spectacular on clear days, but fog and cold are common. Go on A.T. in either direction for a longer hike. Clingmans Dome Rd. is closed Dec. 1–Mar. 31.
  • Length: 0.5 mi. steep
  • Trail type: out and back
  • Skill level: moderate
  • Peak elevation: 6643'

Directions from Asheville:

  1. I-40 west, 18 mi.
  2. Exit 27 US-74 west toward Maggie Valley, 4 mi.
  3. Exit 103 US-19 through Maggie Valley, 12 mi.
  4. Turn right on Blue Ridge Parkway ramp, left on BRP, 13 mi.
  5. Turn right on US-441, 16 mi.
  6. Left on Clingman's Dome Rd. (0.1 mi. before Newfound Gap), 7 mi. to parking.

Lovers Leap Loop

  • Great family hike up to Lovers Leap on the Appalachian Trail. After the river walk, it gets steep but quickly reaches the ridgeline and an outcrop with outstanding views of the French Broad River Valley and the hiker-friendly A.T. Community™ of Hot Springs. Return from this lookout point, or continue on the A.T., take the Silvermine Trail (left turn) down to the USFS campground, turn left and follow the road back out to the parking area at the river.
  • Length: 1.6 mi.
  • Elevation gain: 510'
  • Trail type: Loop (white and orange blazes)
  • Skill level: moderate
  • Trailhead elevation: 1300'

Directions from Asheville:

  1. I-26 north toward Weaverville, 8.4 mi.
  2. Exit 19A US 25/70 past Marshall, Walnut, 21 mi.
  3. Turn left to stay on 25/70 to Hot Springs, <5mi.
  4. Turn right on River Road before French Broad River and Hot springs.
  5. Turn left at T-intersection, go under hwy. to the parking area by the river. The A.T. is straight ahead along the river.
  6. OR stay on 25/70 into Hot Springs, follow white blazes on Main St. north to the trailhead. (This makes a longer hike.)

Max Patch

  • This wide open grassy bald on the Appalachian Trail has stunning views and is tops for a picnic site. You could also follow the white blazes in either direction on the A.T. for a longer hike over the bald and through the woods. Back roads can be difficult in cold or very wet weather.
  • Length: 2.4 mi.
  • Elevation gain: 350'
  • Trail type: Out and back
  • Skill level: easy to moderate
  • Peak elevation: 4,630'

Directions from Asheville:

  1. I-40 west to Exit 7, Harmon Den, +/- 40mi.
  2. Exit, then right on Cold Springs Creek Rd. (becomes Harmon Den Rd., gravel), 6.5 mi.
  3. Sharp left on Max Patch Rd., 2 mi.
  4. Parking area on right. Trail goes left and around, meets the A.T. and up to the summit.
